About Me

Second-year Computer Science student at the University of Liverpool, passionate about low-latency algorithmic systems, high-performance computing, and applied mathematics. I tinker with FPGAs for fun and ship dependable software with a systems mindset.

I have loved software engineering for over a decade. What started as curiosity became a constant pull to understand how things work and turn ideas into real, functioning systems. Writing code feels like exploration, creativity, and problem-solving all at once.

That passion has shaped every opportunity I have and began me gravitating toward challenging environments where learning never stops and responsibility comes early. Outside of work and university, I spend my free time experimenting with hardware and low-level systems, breaking things and rebuilding them better (mostly breaking things). For me, software engineering is something I have grown with for years and intend to keep growing into for the rest of my life.
